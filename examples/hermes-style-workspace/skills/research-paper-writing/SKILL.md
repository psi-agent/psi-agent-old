---

**Paper structure (standard):**

1. **Title & Abstract** — title: specific and searchable. Abstract: problem, method, key result, significance (4–6 sentences).
2. **Introduction** — motivation, gap in literature, contributions (bulleted), paper outline.
3. **Related Work** — group prior work by theme; explain what each group misses that your work addresses.
4. **Method / Approach** — describe your system/algorithm clearly enough to reproduce. Use figures and equations.
5. **Experiments** — datasets, baselines, metrics, results table, ablations.
6. **Discussion** — what the results mean, limitations, failure cases.
7. **Conclusion** — restate contributions, future directions.
8. **References** — BibTeX entries; cite as you write, not after.

**Workflow:**
1. Write the outline first (section headers + one-sentence summaries).
2. Write the experiments section early — results drive the story.
3. Write introduction last (contribution list becomes clear after writing the rest).
4. Iterate: draft → read aloud → revise → repeat.

**Writing rules:**
- One idea per paragraph. First sentence states the idea.
- Avoid "we propose" at the start of every contribution; vary sentence structure.
- Every figure needs a caption that stands alone.
- Every table needs clear column headers with units.
- Spell out acronyms on first use.
